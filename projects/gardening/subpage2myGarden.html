Here is what I grew in 2020!

Kale - Red Russian

  • Season: Direct sowed in late summer for fall/winter harvest.
  • Seeds: Bought from Burpee.com
  • Outcome: Not bitter! Has a light flavor. Grows quickly.
  • Notes for next year: It turned out great so plant again next year! Each plant is small so sow many seeds.

Pea - Super Sugar Snap

  • Season: Direct sowed in last August for fall/winter harvest.
  • Seeds: Bought from Burpee.com
  • Outcome: Very sweet! They are delicious raw directly off the vine. No cooking is necessary.
  • Notes for next year: These are delicious. Plant as early as possible and plant as many as possible. It can be grown in spring as well. Get a better trellis next year. The plants like to sprawl.

Pepper - Jalapeño Early

  • Season: Started indoors in Februrary, transplanted in April, and harvested in summer.
  • Seeds: Bought from Burpee.com
  • Outcome: Very spicy! Too spicy for me but Mom loves them!
  • Notes for next year: Plant less next year. One plant has a lot more peppers than I expected!

Perilla - Kkaennip

  • Season: Started indorrs in February, transplanted in April, and harvested in summer.
  • Seeds: Bought from Kitazawa Seed Company
  • Outcome: Slow start but plants grow huge and last until October
  • Notes for next year: Start as early as possible because it takes a while to get going.

Pumpkin - Rouge Vif d'Etampes

  • Season: Direct sowed in June for fall harvest.
  • Seeds: Bought from Burpee.com
  • Outcome: Only harvested one pumpkin. It is not as big as I would like.
  • Notes for next year: Pay attention to package suggestions on how much space a pumpkin needs! The vines need to spread. If the vines cannot spread, the pumpkins cannot grow very big. Next year, grow smaller varieties of pumpkins instead of this one.

Radish - French Breakfast

  • Season: Direct sowed in spring and last fall.
  • Seeds: Bought from Burpee.com
  • Outcome: There is only one month until harvest. They have a sharp, almost spicy, taste.
  • Notes for next year: Plant less next year. They turned out fine but I discovered that I am not a big fan of radishes.
